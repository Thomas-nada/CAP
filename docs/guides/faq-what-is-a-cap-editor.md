# What is a CAP Editor?

CAP Editors are the appointed cohort responsible for facilitating the CAP process. They are not a governing body — they are process stewards.

---

## What Editors do

- Conduct an **initial review** of every submitted proposal within two weeks of submission, checking for completeness, constitutionality, potential cross-constitutional impacts, and editorial quality
- Ask clarifying questions in the issue comments
- Suggest edits to improve structure or clarity — the author decides whether to accept any suggestion
- Apply **status labels** (Proposed, Accepted, Inactive) to move proposals through the workflow
- Hold regular public meetings (recorded in `CAP-Editors/CAPSLOCK/`) to discuss proposals with authors and the community

## What Editors do not do

- Editors cannot modify your proposal without your consent
- Editors do not vote on whether a proposal is good policy or constitutionally preferable — only whether it is ready and constitutional
- Editors are **not gatekeepers**. An author may withdraw from the CAP process at any time and submit a governance action on-chain directly

## Compensation

CAP Editors may be compensated for their work. Any compensation must be publicly disclosed.

## How to reach Editors

Editor meeting records and contact information are published under `CAP-Editors/CAPSLOCK/` in the repository. You can also engage directly through the GitHub Issue comment thread on your proposal.
