# What happens after deliberation ends?

Nothing happens automatically. The deliberation period expiring is not a trigger — it is a minimum threshold. A CAP Editor must actively review the proposal and decide whether it is ready to move forward.

---

## The steps after deliberation

Once the minimum deliberation period has elapsed:

1. **A CAP Editor reviews** the proposal for completeness, constitutionality, and readiness. They check that the abstract, motivation, specification, and rationale are all present and sufficiently developed.

2. **If it is ready**, the Editor applies the **Proposed** label to the issue. This indicates the proposal is under formal editorial review.

3. **When the Editor is satisfied**, they apply the **Accepted** label.

4. **An automated workflow triggers**: the proposal is committed as a file to the repository under `CAPs/CAP-XXXX/README.md` and a confirmation comment is posted on the issue.

---

## What if the proposal is not ready?

If a proposal has substantive issues — missing information, unresolved concerns from deliberation, or constitutional problems — the Editor will note this in the issue comments. The proposal remains open and the author can continue refining it.

There is no deadline for this process. A proposal can remain in deliberation indefinitely if discussion is still active.

---

## What if the proposal is withdrawn?

Authors may close their issue at any time. A closed issue receives the **Inactive** label and remains in the repository as a permanent historical record. It is not deleted.

---

## After Accepted — what comes next?

Acceptance through the CAP process is not the final step. To make a constitutional change official, an **on-chain governance action** must be submitted to the Cardano Blockchain and approved by at least 65% of the active voting stake.

The CAP process is the off-chain preparation layer. It ensures the proposal is well-formed and community-endorsed before entering the on-chain pipeline. See [How are CAPs approved?](faq-how-are-caps-approved) for the full picture.
