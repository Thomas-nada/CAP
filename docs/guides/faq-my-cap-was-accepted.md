# My CAP was accepted — what now?

Acceptance through the CAP process means your proposal has been formally ratified as community-endorsed and is now committed as a file in the repository. It is a significant milestone — but it is not the last step.

---

## What acceptance means

When a CAP Editor applies the **Accepted** label, an automated workflow commits your proposal to the repository under `CAPs/CAP-XXXX/README.md`. This creates a permanent, citable record. The proposal is now the official community position on this change.

---

## The on-chain step

To make the constitutional change official, a **governance action** must be submitted to the Cardano Blockchain. Your accepted CAP will contain a **Path to Ratification** section that specifies exactly what governance action is required.

You — or anyone else — may submit this governance action. The CAP process does not assign responsibility for on-chain submission to any particular party.

The governance action must be:
- Consistent with the accepted CAP text
- In the standardised format required by the Constitution (URL + document hash + rationale)
- Approved by at least 65% of the active voting stake (or a different threshold if specified in the Guardrails for the specific provision being amended)

---

## What if no one submits the governance action?

An accepted CAP represents community will, but it has no mechanism to force an on-chain vote. If the accepted proposal is not submitted as a governance action, it remains in the repository as a statement of community intent. Anyone may act on it at any time.

If you drafted the CAP and want to see it enacted, the most direct path is to submit the governance action yourself, or coordinate with DReps who support the change to do so.

---

## After the on-chain vote

If the governance action passes, the constitutional change is enacted on-chain. The repository will be updated to reflect the new version of the Constitution, and the amendment history will be updated accordingly.

If the governance action fails, the accepted CAP remains on record. A new CAP or a revised governance action may be submitted in the future.
