# Examples of CAPs

This guide will be updated as proposals move through the process and are ratified. It will highlight well-structured submissions and what made them effective.

---

## CAP-0001 — The CAP Process (Meta)

[CAP-0001](https://github.com/Thomas-nada/CAP/issues/1) defines the CAP process itself. It is the reference document for how all future CAPs should be structured.

It demonstrates:
- A clear **Abstract** that summarises the entire proposal in plain language
- A **Motivation** section that separates the problem from the solution
- A **Specification** that uses structured tables to define categories, periods, and statuses precisely
- A **Rationale** that explains the reasoning behind design choices (e.g., why deliberation periods vary by category)
- A **Path to Ratification** with specific, checkable acceptance criteria

When in doubt about structure or tone, CAP-0001 is the benchmark.
